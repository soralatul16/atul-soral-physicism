Module 1.1: IB Philosophy & Mission

Estimated time: 2 hours | Role relevance: ALL leadership positions

📖 Why This Module Matters

Every single IB interview starts here. If you can't articulate the IB's philosophy fluently, you're done in the first 5 minutes. This isn't about memorizing — it's about believing and living these principles so they come out naturally.

⚠️ In your 21 interviews, you probably answered "What is the IB's mission?" with something generic. Never again.

📖 The IB Mission Statement

Memorize this WORD FOR WORD. Then understand every phrase:

"The International Baccalaureate aims to develop inquiring, knowledgeable and caring young people who help to create a better and more peaceful world through intercultural understanding and respect.

To this end the organization works with schools, governments and international organizations to develop challenging programmes of international education and rigorous assessment.

These programmes encourage students across the world to become active, compassionate and lifelong learners who understand that other people, with their differences, can also be right."

Key phrases to use in interviews:

  • "Inquiring, knowledgeable and caring" — the three pillars. Not just academic (knowledgeable) but curious (inquiring) and human (caring)
  • "Intercultural understanding and respect" — this is International Mindedness in action
  • "Other people, with their differences, can also be right" — this is the heart of open-mindedness. Use this phrase — it impresses interviewers
  • "Active, compassionate and lifelong learners" — active = agency, compassionate = caring, lifelong = beyond school

📖 The IB Learner Profile (10 Attributes)

This is the IB's definition of what an "internationally minded" person looks like. As a coordinator, you must know all 10 AND be able to give examples of how YOUR school develops each one.

  • Inquirers — Nurture curiosity, develop research skills, sustain love of learning. (Not just asking questions — it's structured, purposeful inquiry)
  • Knowledgeable — Develop conceptual understanding across disciplines, engage with local AND global issues. (Not memorization — CONCEPTUAL understanding)
  • Thinkers — Use critical AND creative thinking to analyze complex problems, make ethical decisions. (Both analytical and innovative)
  • Communicators — Express confidently in multiple languages/modes, collaborate effectively, listen to diverse perspectives. (Not just speaking — LISTENING is key)
  • Principled — Act with integrity, honesty, fairness. Take responsibility for actions and consequences. (Link to Academic Honesty policy)
  • Open-minded — Appreciate own AND others' cultures. Seek diverse viewpoints. Grow from experience. (This is International Mindedness at the personal level)
  • Caring — Show empathy, compassion. Commit to service. Make positive difference. (Link to CAS and Service as Action)
  • Balanced — Intellectual, physical AND emotional well-being. Recognize interdependence. (Link to CAS, well-being programs)
  • Reflective — Consider own ideas and experience. Understand strengths and weaknesses for learning and personal development. (Link to ATL self-management)
  • Risk-takers — Approach uncertainty with forethought. Explore new ideas. Resourceful and resilient. (Not reckless — CALCULATED courage)

📖 International Mindedness

This is THE core value of the IB. If you only understand one thing from this course, understand this:

International Mindedness is NOT:

  • ❌ Having students from different countries
  • ❌ Celebrating International Day with food and flags
  • ❌ Teaching about other cultures as "topics"

International Mindedness IS:

  • ✅ Recognizing that multiple perspectives exist on every issue
  • ✅ Understanding our shared humanity AND respecting differences
  • ✅ Developing multilingual competence
  • ✅ Engaging with global issues through local action
  • ✅ Building intercultural understanding as a DAILY practice, not an event

Interview tip: When asked "How do you promote international mindedness?", don't say "We celebrate cultural days." Say: "We embed multiple perspectives into every unit inquiry. For example, when studying energy in physics, students explore how different communities prioritize energy access, sustainability, and economic development differently — there's no single 'right' answer."

📖 The Four IB Programmes

  • PYP (Primary Years Programme) — Ages 3-12. Transdisciplinary. 6 themes. Student agency.
  • MYP (Middle Years Programme) — Ages 11-16. 8 subject groups. Concepts + Global Contexts + ATL. Service as Action.
  • DP (Diploma Programme) — Ages 16-19. 6 subjects + Core (TOK, EE, CAS). External assessment. University preparation.
  • CP (Career-related Programme) — Ages 16-19. DP courses + career-related studies + CP core. Vocational pathway.

Continuum thinking: A coordinator MUST understand how programmes connect. PYP's "transdisciplinary themes" → MYP's "global contexts" → DP's "TOK themes". Service starts in PYP → structured in MYP → CAS in DP.

🔑 Key Vocabulary (Use these in interviews)

International Mindedness
A worldview that recognizes and values diversity of perspective, promotes intercultural understanding, and fosters global engagement.
💬 "Our physics curriculum embeds international mindedness by exploring how different cultures have contributed to scientific understanding."
Learner Profile
Ten attributes that define what it means to be an IB learner. Developed across all four programmes. Central to the IB's definition of an educated person.
💬 "As coordinator, I would ensure the learner profile is visible in unit planning, classroom practice, and school culture — not just on posters."
Conceptual Understanding
Deep understanding of transferable ideas (concepts) that transcend subject boundaries, as opposed to memorization of facts.
💬 "Our assessment design should measure conceptual understanding, not factual recall — students should transfer learning to unfamiliar contexts."
Student Agency
Students' ability to take ownership of their learning through voice, choice, and action. Central to IB pedagogy.
💬 "I promote student agency by offering choice in assessment tasks and involving students in defining inquiry questions."
Approaches to Learning (ATL)
Five categories of skills explicitly taught across subjects: Thinking, Communication, Social, Self-management, Research.
💬 "ATL skills are explicitly planned in every unit — they're not incidental but deliberately developed across the programme."

💬 Interview Practice

Q: "What is the IB's educational philosophy?"
❌ WEAK: "IB focuses on holistic education and producing well-rounded students."
✅ STRONG: "The IB develops internationally minded learners through inquiry-based, concept-driven education. Its mission centers on creating active, compassionate people who recognize that multiple perspectives can coexist. The Learner Profile defines ten attributes — from inquirers to reflective thinkers — that every programme develops. It's not just academic excellence; it's building people who engage ethically with the world."
Q: "How would you promote the Learner Profile in your school?"
❌ WEAK: "We put posters in classrooms and mention it in assemblies."
✅ STRONG: "The Learner Profile should be embedded in three layers: first, in unit planning — every statement of inquiry should naturally develop specific attributes. Second, in assessment feedback — teachers reference LP attributes when commenting on student growth. Third, in school culture — student-led conferences, service programmes, and reflection practices make the LP lived, not displayed."
Q: "How do you develop international mindedness in a school with mostly local students?"
❌ WEAK: "We organize cultural exchange programs and celebrate international days."
✅ STRONG: "International mindedness isn't about passport diversity — it's about perspective diversity. Even in a homogeneous school, we develop it through: multiple perspective inquiry in units, multilingual development, engagement with global issues through local contexts, and teaching students that 'others, with their differences, can also be right.' A physics class exploring energy policy from different nations' economic contexts IS international mindedness."
