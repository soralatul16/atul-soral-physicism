The Three Motion Graphs

Every motion can be described by three linked graphs. Each tells a different part of the story:

Displacement-Time

s vs t

Shows where the object is

Gradient = velocity

Velocity-Time

v vs t

Shows how fast it's going

Gradient = acceleration
Area = displacement

Acceleration-Time

a vs t

Shows how velocity changes

Area = change in velocity
The #1 IB mistake: Confusing what the gradient means on each graph. Gradient of s-t = velocity. Gradient of v-t = acceleration. They are NOT the same thing. Always identify which graph you're reading FIRST.
The connection chain: s-t → (take gradient) → v-t → (take gradient) → a-t. Going the other way: a-t → (take area) → v-t → (take area) → s-t.

Displacement-Time Graph: Reading the Shapes

A: Straight line up → constant velocity (positive direction)
B: Horizontal → at rest (s not changing)
C: Steeper line → faster constant v
D: Curve flattening → decelerating (gradient decreasing)
E: Line going down → returning to start (negative velocity)
Gradient = velocity. Steeper = faster. Flat = stopped. Negative slope = moving backward.

s-t Gradient = Velocity (step by step)

For a straight line (constant velocity):

$$v = \text{gradient} = \frac{\Delta s}{\Delta t} = \frac{s_2 - s_1}{t_2 - t_1}$$

For a curve (changing velocity):

  • Average velocity = gradient of the chord (line connecting two points)
  • Instantaneous velocity = gradient of the tangent (line touching at one point)
FYI: To draw a tangent: place a ruler so it just touches the curve at one point without crossing it. The gradient of that ruler line = instantaneous velocity at that moment.

Velocity-Time Graph: Reading the Shapes

A: Line up from 0 → uniform acceleration from rest  | B: Horizontal → constant velocity (a=0)
C: Line down to 0 → uniform deceleration to rest  | D: Line below axis → accelerating in reverse
E: Horizontal below axis → constant velocity in negative direction
Two key readings: Gradient = acceleration. Area under the line = displacement. Below the axis = negative displacement (moving backward).
A.1 Kinematics  |  A.1.7 Graphs of Motionv-t Gradient & Area

v-t Graph: Two Readings from One Graph

Reading 1: Gradient = Acceleration

$$a = \frac{\Delta v}{\Delta t} = \text{gradient}$$

Reading 2: Area = Displacement

$$s = \text{area under v-t graph}$$
Below the axis: Area below the t-axis counts as negative displacement (object moves backward). Total displacement = positive area − negative area.
Total distance = sum of ALL areas (ignore negative signs). Total displacement = net area (positive − negative).

Acceleration-Time Graph

The a-t graph shows how acceleration varies with time.

Common shapes:

  • Horizontal line at $a > 0$: uniform positive acceleration
  • Horizontal line at $a = 0$: constant velocity (no acceleration)
  • Horizontal line at $a < 0$: uniform deceleration
  • Line at $a = -9.8$: free fall

Area under a-t graph:

$$\text{Area} = \Delta v = \text{change in velocity}$$

This is less commonly asked in IB but connects the graphs together.

Converting Between Graphs

This is the most tested skill. Given one graph, sketch the other two.

Forward (differentiate): Take the gradient → s-t becomes v-t → v-t becomes a-t
Backward (integrate): Take the area → a-t becomes v-t → v-t becomes s-t
IB requires: "From the v-t graph, sketch the corresponding a-t graph" — you must match time intervals, identify gradients for each section, and plot them correctly.

Complete Example: Car Journey

A car accelerates from rest for 4s, travels at constant speed for 3s, then brakes to rest in 3s.

How to convert:
s-t curve (getting steeper) → v-t line going up → a-t positive constant
s-t straight line → v-t horizontal → a-t at zero
s-t curve (getting flatter) → v-t line going down → a-t negative constant
IB Paper 2: "Sketch the v-t graph corresponding to the s-t graph shown." You MUST: match the time intervals, identify whether gradient is +, 0, or −, and check if it's constant or changing.

Context: The v-t graph below shows the motion of a car over 12 seconds.
Calculate [2]

(a) Calculate the acceleration during the first 3 seconds.

$a = \frac{\Delta v}{\Delta t} = \frac{20 - 0}{3} = \mathbf{6.67 \text{ m s}^{-2}}$ [1 formula, 1 answer]
Calculate [3]

(b) Calculate the total displacement of the car over 12 seconds.

Area = trapezium: $s = \frac{1}{2}(b_1 + b_2) \times h = \frac{1}{2}(12 + 6) \times 20$... actually area = triangle + rectangle + triangle:
$= \frac{1}{2}(3)(20) + (6)(20) + \frac{1}{2}(3)(20) = 30 + 120 + 30 = \mathbf{180 \text{ m}}$ [3]
Sketch [3]

(c) Sketch the corresponding a-t graph for the 12-second journey.

0–3s: $a = +6.67$ m/s² (horizontal line above axis) [1]
3–9s: $a = 0$ (line on the axis) [1]
9–12s: $a = \frac{0-20}{3} = -6.67$ m/s² (horizontal line below axis) [1]
Describe [2]

(d) Describe the s-t graph shape for each phase of the journey.

0–3s: Curve getting steeper (increasing gradient = accelerating) [1]
3–9s: Straight line (constant gradient = constant velocity)
9–12s: Curve getting flatter (decreasing gradient = decelerating) [1]

Practice Questions

Determine [2] Medium

On a v-t graph, a straight line goes from (0, 15) to (5, 0). What is the acceleration and total displacement?

$a = \frac{0-15}{5} = \mathbf{-3 \text{ m/s²}}$ (decelerating)
$s = \frac{1}{2} \times 5 \times 15 = \mathbf{37.5 \text{ m}}$ (area of triangle)
Explain [2] Medium

On an s-t graph, the line curves downward (concave down) while s is increasing. What does this tell you about the velocity and acceleration?

The gradient is decreasing → velocity is decreasing [1].
Since velocity is positive but decreasing, acceleration is negative (opposing motion = decelerating) [1].
Calculate [3] Hard

A v-t graph shows: 0–4s velocity = +10 m/s, 4–8s velocity = −5 m/s. Find the total distance and total displacement.

Displacement: $10(4) + (-5)(4) = 40 - 20 = \mathbf{+20 \text{ m}}$
Distance: $|40| + |-20| = 40 + 20 = \mathbf{60 \text{ m}}$
Key: distance = sum of absolute areas. Displacement = net area.
Sketch [3] Hard

An object is thrown vertically upward and returns to the same height. Sketch the s-t, v-t, and a-t graphs for the complete flight.

s-t: Inverted parabola (up, peak, down to 0) [1]
v-t: Straight line from +u through 0 to −u (constant negative gradient) [1]
a-t: Horizontal line at $a = -g$ for entire flight [1]

Key Takeaways

  • s-t graph: gradient = velocity. Straight = constant v. Curve = changing v. Flat = at rest.
  • v-t graph: gradient = acceleration. Area = displacement. Below axis = negative direction.
  • a-t graph: area = change in velocity. Horizontal line = uniform acceleration.
  • Converting forward: take the gradient (s-t → v-t → a-t).
  • Converting backward: take the area (a-t → v-t → s-t).
  • Distance vs displacement from v-t: distance = sum of |areas|. Displacement = net area.
  • Tangent at a point on a curve gives the instantaneous value. Chord gives average.
