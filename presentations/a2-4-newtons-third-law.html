A.2.4

Newton's Third Law

Action & Reaction

Every force has a partner. When you push a wall, the wall pushes you back. When Earth pulls you down, you pull Earth up. Forces always come in pairs — and understanding this changes how you see every interaction.

Atul Soral Physicism

The Law — Every Force Has a Partner

If body A exerts a force on body B, then body B exerts an equal and opposite force on body A.
$$\vec{F}_{A \text{ on } B} = -\vec{F}_{B \text{ on } A}$$

The three rules of Newton's Third Law pairs:

  • Equal in magnitude — always the same size, no exceptions
  • Opposite in direction — point away from each other
  • Act on DIFFERENT objects — this is the key. One on A, one on B. Never on the same object.
The #1 misconception: "If forces are equal and opposite, they cancel and nothing moves." WRONG — they act on DIFFERENT objects, so they can't cancel. Only forces on the SAME object can cancel.
Better name: "Action-reaction" is misleading because it implies one happens first. They're simultaneous — both forces appear and disappear at exactly the same instant. Neither is the "cause."

How to Identify Third Law Pairs

Use the "A on B, B on A" test. Swap the objects and reverse the force:

1Name force as: "[Object A] pushes/pulls [Object B]"
2The pair is: "[Object B] pushes/pulls [Object A]"
3Same type of force, same magnitude, opposite direction, different objects

Examples of valid pairs:

  • Earth pulls book down ↔ Book pulls Earth up
  • Foot pushes ground back ↔ Ground pushes foot forward
  • Hammer hits nail ↔ Nail hits hammer
  • Rocket pushes exhaust down ↔ Exhaust pushes rocket up

NOT valid pairs:

  • Weight and Normal on a book on a table — both act on the same object (the book)
  • These are balanced forces, NOT a third law pair
  • $W$ and $N$ are the same size by Newton's 2nd law ($a = 0$), not by the 3rd law
IB's favourite trick: "Identify the Newton's Third Law pair to the weight of the book." Answer: the gravitational pull of the book on the Earth (NOT the normal force).

Why Don't Action-Reaction Pairs Cancel?

This is the most asked question. If every force has an equal and opposite reaction, how does anything accelerate?

Answer: They act on DIFFERENT objects. You can only add forces on the SAME object to find net force. Third law pairs never act on the same object.

Example: Horse pulls cart

  • Horse pulls cart forward (force on cart)
  • Cart pulls horse backward (force on horse)
  • These are equal and opposite — BUT on different objects

Why does the horse-cart move?

  • Look at cart's FBD: forward pull from horse > backward friction → cart accelerates forward
  • Look at horse's FBD: ground pushes horse forward (friction) > backward pull from cart → horse accelerates forward
The ground is the key. The horse pushes backward on the ground, and the ground pushes the horse forward (friction). This forward force on the horse exceeds the backward pull from the cart, so the horse (and cart) accelerate.

The Classic Trap: Weight and Normal Force

A book sits on a table. Weight acts down, normal acts up. Are they a Newton's Third Law pair?

$$\text{NO! They are NOT a Third Law pair.}$$

Why NOT a pair?

  • Both forces act on the same object (the book)
  • Third law pairs MUST act on different objects
  • $W$ and $N$ are equal because of Newton's 2nd law ($a = 0$), not 3rd law
  • In an elevator accelerating up: $N > W$. They're NOT equal → can't be a 3rd law pair

The actual pairs:

  • Pair 1: Earth pulls book down ↔ Book pulls Earth up
  • Pair 2: Table pushes book up ↔ Book pushes table down
  • Four forces total, two separate pairs, involving three objects (book, Earth, table)
Quick test: A valid pair always involves exactly TWO objects and the SAME type of force. Weight (gravitational) pairs with gravitational. Normal (contact) pairs with contact.

How the Third Law Makes Things Move

Every method of locomotion relies on Newton's Third Law:

🚶

Walking

Foot pushes ground backward → Ground pushes foot forward

Without friction (ice), foot slides back — can't walk

🏊

Swimming

Hands push water backward → Water pushes swimmer forward

Bigger hands/fins → more water pushed → faster

🚀

Rocket

Rocket pushes exhaust down → Exhaust pushes rocket up

Works in vacuum — doesn't need air to push against

Common thread: To move forward, you must push something backward. The reaction to your backward push is the forward force that propels you.
Driving a car: Engine spins wheels. Wheels push road backward (friction). Road pushes car forward. On ice (no friction), wheels spin but car doesn't move — no reaction force.

Worked Example — Identify All Pairs 6/10

A 2 kg ball rests on the floor. Identify all Newton's Third Law pairs involving the ball.

Forces ON the ball:

  • Weight $W$ = 19.6 N downward (Earth pulls ball)
  • Normal $N$ = 19.6 N upward (Floor pushes ball)

Third Law pairs:

1Pair A: Earth pulls ball down (19.6 N) ↔ Ball pulls Earth up (19.6 N)

Same type: gravitational. Different objects: ball and Earth.

2Pair B: Floor pushes ball up (19.6 N) ↔ Ball pushes floor down (19.6 N)

Same type: contact/normal. Different objects: ball and floor.

$W$ and $N$ are NOT a pair — they both act on the ball. They're equal here because $a = 0$ (Newton's 2nd law), not because of the 3rd law.
Does Earth really accelerate upward? Yes — by $a = F/M = 19.6/(6 \times 10^{24}) = 3.3 \times 10^{-24}$ m/s². Immeasurably small, but real.

IB-Style Question Set 8/10

Context: A person of mass 70 kg stands on a bathroom scale inside an elevator. The scale reads 750 N.
State [1]

(a) State Newton's Third Law.

If body A exerts a force on body B, then body B exerts an equal and opposite force on body A. (The forces are the same type, act on different objects, are simultaneous.) [1]
Identify [2]

(b) The scale exerts a normal force of 750 N upward on the person. Identify the Newton's Third Law pair to this force.

The person pushes the scale downward with a force of 750 N [1]. Same type (contact/normal), same magnitude, opposite direction, different objects (person and scale) [1].
Determine [2]

(c) Determine the acceleration of the elevator and state its direction.

$N - mg = ma$: $750 - 686 = 70a$ → $a = 64/70 = \mathbf{0.91 \text{ m s}^{-2}}$ upward [2]
(Scale reads more than weight → accelerating upward)
Explain [2]

(d) The student says "the normal force and weight are a Newton's Third Law pair." Explain why this is incorrect.

Both forces act on the same object (the person) [1]. Newton's Third Law pairs must act on different objects. Also, $N \neq W$ here (750 ≠ 686), so they can't be "equal and opposite" as the 3rd law requires [1].
Identify [2]

(e) Identify the Newton's Third Law pair to the person's weight.

The gravitational pull of the person on the Earth [1]. Same type (gravitational), magnitude 686 N, directed upward toward the person, acting on the Earth [1].

Practice Questions

Identify [2] Easy

You push a box across the floor. State the Newton's Third Law pair to the force you exert on the box.

The box pushes you backward with an equal force [1]. Same magnitude, opposite direction, contact force, acts on you (not the box) [1].
Explain [3] Medium

If action and reaction forces are always equal, explain how a tennis racket can accelerate a ball.

The racket exerts a force on the ball AND the ball exerts an equal force on the racket [1]. But these act on different objects. The ball accelerates a lot (small mass) while the racket decelerates only slightly (larger mass + held by arm) [1]. Net force on each object determines its acceleration independently via $F = ma$ [1].
Explain [2] Hard

Explain how a rocket accelerates in empty space where there is nothing to push against.

The rocket pushes exhaust gases backward [1]. By Newton's Third Law, the exhaust pushes the rocket forward. The rocket doesn't push against air or ground — it pushes against its own exhaust. This works in vacuum because the reaction pair is between the rocket and the exhaust, not between the rocket and any external medium [1].
Explain [3] Hard

A small car collides head-on with a large truck. Compare the forces each exerts on the other and explain why the car suffers more damage.

By Newton's 3rd Law, the force the car exerts on the truck equals the force the truck exerts on the car [1]. But by $F = ma$, the car (smaller mass) experiences a much larger acceleration/deceleration [1]. Greater deceleration means greater damage to the car and its occupants — the force is the same, but the effect depends on mass [1].

Key Takeaways

  • $F_{A \text{ on } B} = -F_{B \text{ on } A}$ — equal magnitude, opposite direction, different objects.
  • Same type of force — gravitational pairs with gravitational, contact with contact.
  • Simultaneous — both appear and disappear together. Neither is "first."
  • They DON'T cancel — because they act on different objects.
  • Weight and Normal are NOT a pair — they both act on the same object.
  • Locomotion: push something backward → it pushes you forward (walking, swimming, rockets).
  • Car vs truck: same force on each, but car has more $a$ because less mass → more damage.
