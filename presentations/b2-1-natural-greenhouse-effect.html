B.2.1

The Natural Greenhouse Effect

Why Earth Isn't a Frozen Rock

Without the greenhouse effect, Earth's surface would average −18°C. Oceans would freeze solid. Life as we know it would be impossible. The natural greenhouse effect is not a problem — it is a prerequisite for life. Understanding the physics of how it works is the first step to understanding why its enhancement is dangerous.

The Two Critical Physics Facts

The entire greenhouse effect rests on two facts about electromagnetic radiation:

FACT 1: WIEN'S DISPLACEMENT LAW

Hotter objects emit radiation at shorter wavelengths. The Sun (5800 K) peaks in visible light (~500 nm). The Earth (288 K) peaks in infrared (~10 μm). This 20× difference in peak wavelength is the entire reason the greenhouse effect exists.

$\lambda_{\max,\text{Sun}} = \frac{2.90 \times 10^{-3}}{5800} = 500$ nm (visible)

$\lambda_{\max,\text{Earth}} = \frac{2.90 \times 10^{-3}}{288} = 10.1$ μm (infrared)

FACT 2: SELECTIVE ABSORPTION BY THE ATMOSPHERE

The atmosphere is largely transparent to visible light (solar radiation passes through) but partially opaque to infrared radiation (Earth's radiation is absorbed). This one-way filter is what traps energy.

Visible light: atmosphere transmits ~75%. Infrared: atmosphere absorbs ~70-85% (depending on wavelength and cloud cover).

This wavelength asymmetry is everything. If the atmosphere were equally transparent to all wavelengths, there would be no greenhouse effect. If it were equally opaque to all, the Sun's light wouldn't reach the surface. The fact that it's transparent to short-λ and opaque to long-λ creates the energy trap.

The Natural Greenhouse Effect — Complete Mechanism

① Short-wavelength solar radiation (mostly visible, 400–700 nm) arrives at Earth. The atmosphere is transparent to these wavelengths — they pass straight through to the surface.

② About 30% is reflected by clouds, ice, and light surfaces (albedo). The remaining 70% (~950 W m⁻²) is absorbed by the surface.

③ The surface warms and re-emits radiation. By Wien's law, at 288 K this peaks at ~10 μm — infrared, 20× longer wavelength than sunlight.

④⑤ Greenhouse gas molecules (CO₂, H₂O, CH₄, N₂O) absorb this infrared radiation. Their molecular bonds vibrate at these exact frequencies. They then re-emit IR in all directions — including back toward the surface.

⑥⑦ Some IR escapes to space through "atmospheric windows" (wavelengths where no GHG absorbs). The system reaches equilibrium when total energy radiated to space = total energy absorbed from the Sun. This equilibrium occurs at 288 K — not 255 K — because the GHGs add an extra 33 K.

Earth's Energy Budget — The Numbers

For every 100 units of solar energy arriving at the top of Earth's atmosphere:

INCOMING (100 units = 1361 W m⁻²)
Reflected by clouds & atmosphere~22 units
Reflected by surface (ice, sand, etc.)~8 units
Absorbed by atmosphere (ozone, dust, aerosols)~23 units
Absorbed by Earth's surface~47 units
OUTGOING (must also total 100 for equilibrium)
Reflected back (albedo total)~30 units
IR radiated directly from surface through atmospheric window~12 units
IR radiated by atmosphere/clouds to space~58 units
Only ~12% of Earth's IR escapes directly to space through the "atmospheric window" (8–13 μm, where no major GHG absorbs strongly). The other ~88% is intercepted by GHGs, re-emitted multiple times, and eventually leaves from higher, colder layers of the atmosphere. This is why the surface is warmer than a simple calculation predicts.
The atmosphere radiates ~340 W m⁻² back to the surface — more than the surface receives directly from the Sun (~240 W m⁻²). The greenhouse "blanket" delivers more energy to the ground than direct sunlight does. This is measured fact, not theory.

Calculating Earth's Temperature — With and Without GHGs

WITHOUT greenhouse gases (bare rock)

Power in = Power out: $S\pi R^2(1-\alpha) = \sigma(4\pi R^2)T^4$

$T = \left(\frac{S(1-\alpha)}{4\sigma}\right)^{1/4} = \left(\frac{1361 \times 0.70}{4 \times 5.67 \times 10^{-8}}\right)^{1/4}$

$T = 255$ K $= -18°C$ — frozen, uninhabitable

WITH natural greenhouse gases (actual Earth)

The GHGs add an insulating layer. The effective radiating temperature to space is still ~255 K, but this radiation now comes from higher in the atmosphere (typically ~5 km altitude), not the surface.

Since temperature decreases with altitude (lapse rate ~6.5 K/km), the surface must be warmer:

$T_{\text{surface}} = 255 + (6.5 \times 5) = 255 + 33 = 288$ K $= +15°C$ — habitable!

The 33 K greenhouse warming is the difference between −18°C and +15°C. This is the natural greenhouse effect. It has been operating for billions of years and is essential for life. Water contributes ~21 K, CO₂ ~7 K, ozone ~2.5 K, N₂O + CH₄ ~2.5 K.
Why the simple model uses $\pi R^2$ for absorption but $4\pi R^2$ for emission: Earth intercepts sunlight over its cross-section (a circle of area $\pi R^2$), but it radiates heat from its entire spherical surface ($4\pi R^2$). The factor of 4 accounts for this geometry.

The Atmospheric Window

The atmosphere isn't uniformly opaque to IR. There are specific wavelength bands where each GHG absorbs, and gaps where IR passes through freely.

The atmospheric window (8–13 μm) is the wavelength range where no major greenhouse gas absorbs strongly. Earth's peak emission (~10 μm) falls right in this window — which is why ~12% of surface IR escapes directly to space.

Why CO₂ matters at 15 μm: CO₂'s main absorption band is centred at 15 μm. As CO₂ concentration increases, this band widens and also encroaches on the edges of the atmospheric window, closing it further. This is the physical mechanism by which more CO₂ traps more heat.
CH₄ and N₂O absorb in the window region (around 7–8 μm for CH₄). Even though their concentrations are tiny, they absorb at wavelengths where no other gas does — making each molecule disproportionately effective. This is why CH₄ is ~80× more potent than CO₂ per molecule over 20 years.

Venus is the cautionary tale. Its CO₂ concentration is 2,400× Earth's. A runaway greenhouse effect raised surface temperature to 735 K — hot enough to melt lead. Venus once had liquid water; the greenhouse effect boiled it all away. This demonstrates that the greenhouse effect can be catastrophic if the atmosphere's composition changes enough.
Mars proves that CO₂ alone isn't enough — you also need atmospheric pressure. Mars is 96% CO₂ but its atmosphere is only 0.6% as thick as Earth's, so the total amount of CO₂ is too small to trap much heat. Concentration × pressure = total greenhouse effect.

The Atmospheric Temperature Profile

Temperature decreases with altitude in the troposphere (lowest ~12 km). This is called the environmental lapse rate — about 6.5 K per km.

Why GHGs warm the surface — the altitude explanation

Earth must radiate 255 K worth of energy to space to balance solar input. GHGs make the lower atmosphere opaque to IR, so the radiation that escapes comes from higher up — where it's colder. The effective emission height is ~5 km.

Since temperature drops 6.5 K per km, the surface must be $5 \times 6.5 = 33$ K warmer than the emission altitude: $255 + 33 = 288$ K.

More GHGs → emission height rises → surface must be even warmer to compensate → global warming.

This is the most physically complete explanation of the greenhouse effect. It avoids the common (but incomplete) analogy of a "blanket." The real mechanism is: GHGs raise the altitude from which Earth radiates to space, and the lapse rate then requires the surface to be warmer.

Outline [4]

(a) Outline the mechanism by which the natural greenhouse effect maintains Earth's surface temperature above the expected black-body temperature.

Short-wavelength solar radiation (visible/UV) passes through the atmosphere, which is largely transparent to these wavelengths, and is absorbed by Earth's surface [1]. The warmed surface re-emits longer-wavelength infrared radiation (peak ~10 μm) [1]. Greenhouse gases (CO₂, H₂O, CH₄) in the atmosphere absorb this IR radiation because their molecular vibrational frequencies match these wavelengths [1]. The GHG molecules re-emit IR in all directions, including back toward the surface, providing additional warming that raises the equilibrium surface temperature from 255 K to 288 K [1].
Calculate [3]

(b) Earth receives solar radiation with intensity 1361 W m⁻² and has albedo 0.30. Calculate the expected surface temperature without an atmosphere.

$T = \left(\frac{S(1-\alpha)}{4\sigma}\right)^{1/4} = \left(\frac{1361 \times 0.70}{4 \times 5.67 \times 10^{-8}}\right)^{1/4}$ [1]
$= \left(\frac{952.7}{2.268 \times 10^{-7}}\right)^{1/4} = (4.20 \times 10^9)^{1/4}$ [1]
$T = \mathbf{255 \text{ K}}$ [1]
Explain [3]

(c) Explain why the atmosphere is transparent to incoming solar radiation but partially opaque to outgoing terrestrial radiation.

Solar radiation peaks at ~500 nm (visible light). The main atmospheric gases (N₂, O₂) and greenhouse gases do not have molecular vibration modes that absorb at these wavelengths, so visible light passes through [1]. Earth's surface radiation peaks at ~10 μm (infrared). Greenhouse gas molecules have bond vibration frequencies that match these IR wavelengths — they absorb energy by transitioning between vibrational energy states [1]. This wavelength-dependent absorption (transparent to short-λ, opaque to long-λ) creates the one-way energy trap that is the greenhouse effect [1].
Explain [2]

(d) Explain what is meant by the "atmospheric window" and its significance.

The atmospheric window is the wavelength range (~8–13 μm) where no major greenhouse gas absorbs strongly, allowing IR radiation from Earth's surface to pass directly to space [1]. It is significant because it provides the primary direct cooling mechanism for the surface — about 12% of terrestrial IR escapes through this window. Increasing concentrations of certain GHGs (especially CH₄ and CFCs) can partially close this window, reducing Earth's ability to cool [1].

More Practice 8/10

Calculate [3]

The Moon has no atmosphere and an average albedo of 0.12. Using the solar constant 1361 W m⁻², calculate the Moon's expected average surface temperature. Compare with Earth's no-atmosphere temperature and explain the difference.

$T = \left(\frac{1361 \times 0.88}{4 \times 5.67 \times 10^{-8}}\right)^{1/4} = \left(\frac{1197.7}{2.268 \times 10^{-7}}\right)^{1/4}$ [1]
$= (5.28 \times 10^9)^{1/4} = \mathbf{270 \text{ K}}$ [1]
This is higher than Earth's 255 K because the Moon reflects less sunlight (lower albedo) — it absorbs more solar energy. But Earth's actual temperature (288 K) exceeds the Moon's because Earth's atmosphere provides 33 K of greenhouse warming [1].
Discuss [4]

Explain why the statement "the greenhouse effect is bad for the planet" is scientifically inaccurate. Distinguish between the natural greenhouse effect and the enhanced greenhouse effect.

The natural greenhouse effect is essential for life — without it, Earth would be −18°C and entirely frozen [1]. It has maintained habitable conditions for billions of years through a stable concentration of GHGs [1]. The enhanced greenhouse effect refers to the additional warming caused by human emissions of CO₂, CH₄, and other GHGs beyond natural levels [1]. It is the rate and magnitude of this enhancement — not the greenhouse effect itself — that causes climate change. The physics is the same mechanism; only the quantity of GHGs differs [1].
Explain [3]

Using the lapse rate model, explain why increasing the concentration of greenhouse gases leads to surface warming.

More GHGs make the lower atmosphere more opaque to IR radiation, forcing the effective emission height (the altitude from which Earth's radiation escapes to space) to move upward [1]. Since temperature decreases with altitude (lapse rate ~6.5 K/km), moving the emission height up means the radiation escapes from a colder layer [1]. To maintain energy balance (same total power radiated to space = same effective temperature at the emission height), the entire temperature profile must shift upward — meaning the surface temperature increases [1].
Historical note: The greenhouse effect was first described by Joseph Fourier (1824), quantified by Svante Arrhenius (1896, who calculated that doubling CO₂ would warm Earth by ~5°C — remarkably close to modern estimates of 2.5–4°C), and experimentally confirmed by John Tyndall (1859, who showed CO₂ and H₂O absorb IR). The physics has been understood for over 150 years.

Key Takeaways

  • Sun emits short-λ (visible) which passes through atmosphere. Earth emits long-λ (IR) which GHGs absorb.
  • This wavelength asymmetry is the entire reason the greenhouse effect exists.
  • Without GHE: 255 K (−18°C). With natural GHE: 288 K (+15°C). Difference: 33 K.
  • Energy budget: ~47% of solar absorbed by surface. ~88% of surface IR intercepted by GHGs. ~12% escapes through atmospheric window (8–13 μm).
  • Lapse rate model: GHGs raise the effective emission height. Surface must be warmer by (height × 6.5 K/km).
  • Atmospheric window (8–13 μm): the gap where IR escapes. CO₂ (15 μm), H₂O (various), CH₄ (7–8 μm) absorb outside and at edges of this window.
  • Venus: 96% CO₂ → +505 K greenhouse warming → 735 K surface. Runaway greenhouse is real.
  • The natural GHE is essential for life. The problem is its enhancement by human emissions.
