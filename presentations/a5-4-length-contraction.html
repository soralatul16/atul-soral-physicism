A.5.4

Length Contraction

Moving Objects Are Shorter

A metre stick flying past you at 0.9c would appear only 44 cm long. The stick hasn't been compressed — space itself has contracted in the direction of motion. The faster you go, the shorter the universe becomes.

Atul Soral Physicism

A.5 Galilean & Special Relativity  |  A.5.4 Length ContractionThe Formula

Length Contraction

An object moving relative to an observer is measured to be shorter in the direction of motion than its rest length.

$$L = \frac{L_0}{\gamma} = L_0\sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}$$

L₀ (Proper Length)

Length measured in the frame where the object is at rest. Always the longest measurement.

L (Contracted Length)

Length measured by an observer who sees the object moving. Always shorter: $L < L_0$.

Only in the direction of motion. A spaceship moving along the x-axis contracts in x, but its height and width stay the same. A sphere becomes a pancake, not a smaller sphere.

Proper Length — The Key Concept

PROPER LENGTH (L₀)

The length of an object measured in the frame where the object is at rest. It is the longest possible measurement of that object.

Proper time vs Proper length — don't confuse them!

Proper time Δt₀
Measured by clock at both events
Is the shortest time
$\Delta t = \gamma\Delta t_0$ (multiply by γ)
Proper length L₀
Measured in object's rest frame
Is the longest length
$L = L_0/\gamma$ (divide by γ)
Memory trick: Time dilates (gets bigger by γ). Length contracts (gets smaller by γ). They go in opposite directions. Proper time is smallest; proper length is largest.

How to identify proper length

  • The object is stationary in that frame
  • Both ends are measured at the same time in that frame (trivially)
  • It's the "ruler lying on the table" measurement

Deriving Length Contraction from Time Dilation

Consider a muon travelling from the top of the atmosphere to the ground at speed $v$.

Earth's frame

Distance = $L_0$ (proper length of atmosphere)

Time = $\Delta t = \gamma\Delta t_0$ (dilated)

$L_0 = v\Delta t = v\gamma\Delta t_0$

Muon's frame

Distance = $L$ (contracted atmosphere)

Time = $\Delta t_0$ (proper time)

$L = v\Delta t_0$

Dividing: $\frac{L}{L_0} = \frac{v\Delta t_0}{v\gamma\Delta t_0} = \frac{1}{\gamma}$

$$L = \frac{L_0}{\gamma}$$
Both explanations are equally valid:
• Earth says: "The muon's clock runs slow, so it lives long enough to reach us" (time dilation).
• Muon says: "The atmosphere is shorter, so I reach the ground before I decay" (length contraction).
Same physics, different perspectives.

Worked Examples — Step by Step

Example 1: Spaceship

A spaceship has a rest length of 120 m. It passes Earth at 0.85c. What length does an Earth observer measure?

Identify: $L_0 = 120$ m (ship's rest frame), $v = 0.85c$

$\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - 0.7225}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{0.2775}} = \frac{1}{0.5268} = 1.898$

$L = \frac{L_0}{\gamma} = \frac{120}{1.898} = \mathbf{63.2 \text{ m}}$ — almost half its rest length!

Example 2: Finding speed from contraction

A rod has rest length 1.00 m. A lab observer measures it as 0.60 m. How fast is the rod moving?

$L = \frac{L_0}{\gamma}$ → $\gamma = \frac{L_0}{L} = \frac{1.00}{0.60} = 1.667$

$\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-v^2/c^2}}$ → $1 - v^2/c^2 = \frac{1}{\gamma^2} = 0.36$ → $v^2/c^2 = 0.64$

$v = \mathbf{0.80c}$

Exam tip: Finding $v$ from γ: $v = c\sqrt{1 - 1/\gamma^2}$. This rearrangement appears in both time dilation and length contraction problems.

The Muon Problem — Two Perspectives

A muon is created 15 km above Earth, travelling at 0.995c. Its rest lifetime is 2.2 μs. Does it reach the ground?

Earth's Frame (time dilation)

$\gamma = 10.0$

Dilated lifetime: $\Delta t = 10 \times 2.2 = 22$ μs

Distance travelled: $0.995c \times 22 \times 10^{-6}$

$= 6570$ m ≈ 6.6 km

Not quite 15 km, but many more muons reach the surface than classical physics predicts.

Muon's Frame (length contraction)

$\gamma = 10.0$

Contracted atmosphere: $L = \frac{15000}{10} = 1500$ m

Time to traverse: $\frac{1500}{0.995c} = 5.0$ μs

5.0 μs > 2.2 μs: most muons still decay, but far more survive than classically expected.

Both frames give the same physical prediction: significantly more muons reach the ground than Newtonian physics predicts. The observation matches special relativity perfectly. The two explanations (time dilation in Earth's frame, length contraction in muon's frame) are complementary — not competing.

What Contracts and What Doesn't

Contracts (in direction of motion)

  • Length of the object along the direction of travel
  • Distances between objects along the direction of travel
  • The length of a race track as seen by a moving car
  • The thickness of the atmosphere as seen by a muon

Does NOT contract

  • Dimensions perpendicular to motion (height, width)
  • The object in its own rest frame
  • Time (time dilates — it doesn't contract!)
  • Mass, charge, or any scalar quantity
It's not optical squashing. Length contraction is not what you would "see" with your eyes (that involves light travel time effects and looks like rotation — Terrell rotation). Length contraction is what you would measure with carefully synchronised clocks and rulers.

(a) Define proper length.

Proper length is the length of an object measured in the reference frame in which the object is at rest [1].
Calculate [3]

(b) A rocket has a proper length of 50.0 m and travels at 0.75c relative to Earth. Calculate the length measured by an Earth observer.

$\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-0.5625}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{0.4375}} = \frac{1}{0.6614} = 1.512$ [1]
$L = \frac{L_0}{\gamma} = \frac{50.0}{1.512}$ [1]
$L = \mathbf{33.1 \text{ m}}$ [1]
Calculate [3]

(c) A metre stick passes an observer at high speed. The observer measures its length as 0.50 m. Calculate the speed of the metre stick.

$\gamma = \frac{L_0}{L} = \frac{1.00}{0.50} = 2.0$ [1]
$v = c\sqrt{1 - 1/\gamma^2} = c\sqrt{1 - 0.25} = c\sqrt{0.75}$ [1]
$v = \mathbf{0.866c}$ [1]
Explain [3]

(d) A muon travels through 15 km of atmosphere at 0.995c ($\gamma = 10$). Explain how length contraction in the muon's frame accounts for it reaching the ground, and compare with the time dilation explanation in Earth's frame.

In the muon's frame, the atmosphere is contracted: $L = 15000/10 = 1500$ m [1]. The muon only needs to travel 1500 m, which takes $\sim$5 μs at 0.995c — comparable to its 2.2 μs lifetime, so many survive [1]. In Earth's frame, the muon's lifetime is dilated to $10 \times 2.2 = 22$ μs, giving it time to travel much further. Both explanations predict the same number of muons reaching the surface [1].

Calculate [3]

An astronaut travels to a star 8.0 light-years away at 0.90c. What is the distance to the star in the astronaut's frame?

$\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-0.81}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{0.19}} = 2.294$ [1]
$L = \frac{L_0}{\gamma} = \frac{8.0}{2.294}$ [1]
$L = \mathbf{3.49 \text{ light-years}}$ [1]
The astronaut perceives the star as being only 3.49 ly away!
Calculate [3]

A proton in the LHC has γ = 7000. The LHC ring has a circumference of 27 km. What is the circumference of the ring in the proton's frame?

$L = \frac{L_0}{\gamma} = \frac{27000}{7000}$ [1]
$L = \mathbf{3.86 \text{ m}}$ [1]
The proton "sees" the entire 27 km ring contracted to less than 4 metres! [1]
Explain [2]

A cube moves at 0.8c along the x-axis. Its rest dimensions are 1.0 m × 1.0 m × 1.0 m. Describe its dimensions as measured by a stationary observer.

$\gamma = 1.667$. The dimension along the direction of motion (x) contracts: $L_x = 1.0/1.667 = 0.60$ m [1]. The dimensions perpendicular to motion (y and z) are unchanged: 1.0 m each. The cube appears as a rectangular prism: $0.60 \times 1.0 \times 1.0$ m [1].
Thought experiment: If you could travel at 0.9999c toward Andromeda (2.5 million light-years away), length contraction would shrink the distance to ~35,000 light-years in your frame. At that speed, you'd arrive in about 35,000 years of your own time — not 2.5 million. Relativistic space travel is "possible" in principle, but the energy requirements are astronomical.

Key Takeaways

  • $L = L_0/\gamma$ — moving objects are shorter in the direction of motion.
  • Proper length ($L_0$): measured in the object's rest frame. Always the longest.
  • Only along direction of motion. Perpendicular dimensions are unaffected.
  • Derived from time dilation — both give the same physical predictions (muon problem).
  • Complementary explanations: Earth uses time dilation, muon uses length contraction. Same result.
  • Finding speed: $v = c\sqrt{1 - L^2/L_0^2}$ or equivalently $v = c\sqrt{1 - 1/\gamma^2}$.
  • Not visual squashing — it's what you measure, not what you optically see (Terrell rotation).
