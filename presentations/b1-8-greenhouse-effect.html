B.1.8

Earth's Energy Balance

& the Greenhouse Effect

Without the greenhouse effect, Earth's average temperature would be −18°C — a frozen, lifeless world. The greenhouse effect keeps us at a comfortable +15°C. But too much of it, from burning fossil fuels, is now overheating the planet. This is the physics of climate change.

Earth's Energy Balance

For Earth's temperature to remain stable, energy in must equal energy out:

$$P_{\text{absorbed}} = P_{\text{radiated}}$$
The wavelength shift is crucial: Sun emits mostly short-wavelength radiation (visible, UV) which passes through the atmosphere. Earth, being much cooler, re-emits long-wavelength infrared radiation — which greenhouse gases absorb. This asymmetry is the basis of the greenhouse effect.

The Greenhouse Effect — Step by Step

The IB explanation (memorise): (1) Short-wave solar radiation passes through the atmosphere and is absorbed by Earth's surface. (2) The surface warms and re-emits longer-wavelength infrared radiation. (3) Greenhouse gases absorb this IR radiation. (4) The GHGs re-emit IR in all directions — including back toward the surface. (5) This "traps" energy, warming the surface above what it would be without GHGs.

N₂ and O₂ are NOT greenhouse gases. They make up 99% of the atmosphere but don't absorb infrared radiation — their molecular structure (diatomic, symmetric) doesn't interact with IR. Only molecules with 3+ atoms (or asymmetric bonds) absorb IR by changing their vibrational modes.
Water vapour is the biggest greenhouse gas but we can't directly control it. It acts as a feedback: warming increases evaporation → more H₂O → more warming. CO₂ is the primary forcing agent because we directly control its emissions.

Natural vs Enhanced Greenhouse Effect

Natural (essential for life)

  • Pre-industrial CO₂ ≈ 280 ppm
  • Warms Earth by ~33 K (−18°C → +15°C)
  • Without it: frozen oceans, no liquid water
  • Has been stable for thousands of years

Enhanced (human-caused problem)

  • Current CO₂ ≈ 424 ppm (50% increase)
  • Extra warming: ~1.2°C so far
  • Caused by burning fossil fuels + deforestation
  • Rate of increase is unprecedented

The mechanism of enhancement

More CO₂ → more IR absorbed in the atmosphere → more re-emitted back to the surface → surface warms → emits more IR → but still more is trapped → new equilibrium at a higher temperature.

The energy balance shifts: $P_{\text{in}} > P_{\text{out}}$ temporarily → Earth warms until $P_{\text{out}}$ catches up (at a higher T, since $P_{\text{out}} \propto T^4$).

IB distinction: "Greenhouse effect" = natural, necessary, and beneficial. "Enhanced greenhouse effect" = the additional warming caused by human-produced greenhouse gases. The IB expects you to use these terms precisely.

Albedo & Feedback Mechanisms

Albedo (α)

The fraction of incoming solar radiation that is reflected by a surface. $\alpha = 0$ (absorbs all) to $\alpha = 1$ (reflects all).

Fresh snow
0.80–0.90
Clouds
0.50–0.70
Desert sand
0.30–0.40
Ocean
0.06–0.10

Earth's average albedo ≈ 0.30 (30% of sunlight reflected back to space).

Ice-Albedo Feedback (positive feedback loop)

Warming → ice melts → less reflective surface exposed (ocean/land) → more solar absorption → more warming → more ice melts → ...

This is a runaway effect — it accelerates itself. It's why the Arctic is warming 2–3× faster than the global average.

Water vapour feedback (also positive)

Warming → more evaporation → more H₂O in atmosphere → stronger greenhouse effect → more warming. This roughly doubles the warming from CO₂ alone.

Positive feedback ≠ good. In physics, "positive" means self-amplifying. A positive feedback makes changes bigger. The ice-albedo and water vapour feedbacks both amplify warming.

Consequences of Enhanced Greenhouse Effect

Rising sea levels

Thermal expansion of ocean water + melting of ice sheets (Greenland, Antarctica). Currently ~3.6 mm/year. Threatens coastal cities, island nations.

Extreme weather

More energy in the atmosphere → more intense storms, heatwaves, droughts, and flooding. Weather patterns become less predictable.

Ocean acidification

CO₂ dissolves in seawater forming carbonic acid. pH has dropped ~0.1 since 1800. Threatens coral reefs, shellfish, marine food chains.

Ecosystem disruption

Species can't adapt fast enough. Coral bleaching, shifting habitats, desertification, loss of biodiversity. Permafrost melting releases more CH₄.

TOK connection: The physics of the greenhouse effect is not controversial — it's well-established since Arrhenius (1896). The science of climate change is one of the most tested and confirmed theories in modern physics. IB expects you to distinguish between the science (certain) and the political response (debated).

IB-Style Question Set 7/10

Outline [3]

(a) Outline the mechanism of the greenhouse effect.

Short-wavelength solar radiation (visible/UV) passes through the atmosphere and is absorbed by Earth's surface, warming it [1]. The warm surface re-emits longer-wavelength infrared radiation [1]. Greenhouse gases (CO₂, H₂O, CH₄) absorb this IR radiation and re-emit it in all directions, including back toward the surface, raising the surface temperature above what it would be without an atmosphere [1].
Distinguish [2]

(b) Distinguish between the natural greenhouse effect and the enhanced greenhouse effect.

The natural greenhouse effect is caused by naturally occurring greenhouse gases and raises Earth's temperature by ~33 K to a habitable ~288 K [1]. The enhanced greenhouse effect is the additional warming caused by increased concentrations of greenhouse gases due to human activities (burning fossil fuels, deforestation), leading to global warming beyond the natural level [1].
Explain [3]

(c) Explain the ice-albedo feedback mechanism and why it is described as a positive feedback loop.

As temperatures rise, ice and snow melt, exposing darker ocean or land surfaces which have a lower albedo [1]. These darker surfaces absorb more solar radiation, causing further warming [1]. This additional warming melts more ice, reinforcing the cycle. It is a positive feedback because the initial change (warming) is amplified by the feedback mechanism, making the warming progressively stronger [1].
Explain [2]

(d) Explain why N₂ and O₂ are not greenhouse gases despite making up 99% of the atmosphere.

N₂ and O₂ are diatomic molecules with symmetric bonds [1]. They do not have the vibrational modes needed to absorb and re-emit infrared radiation. Greenhouse gases are molecules with three or more atoms (CO₂, H₂O, CH₄) or asymmetric bonds that can vibrate at IR frequencies [1].

More Practice 8/10

Calculate [3]

If Earth's albedo increased from 0.30 to 0.35 (e.g., more cloud cover), calculate the new equilibrium temperature. ($S = 1361$ W m⁻²)

$T = \left(\frac{S(1-\alpha)}{4\sigma}\right)^{1/4} = \left(\frac{1361 \times 0.65}{4 \times 5.67 \times 10^{-8}}\right)^{1/4}$ [1]
$= \left(\frac{884.7}{2.268 \times 10^{-7}}\right)^{1/4} = (3.90 \times 10^9)^{1/4}$ [1]
$= \mathbf{250 \text{ K} = -23°C}$ (5 K cooler than with α = 0.30) [1]
Discuss [4]

Discuss why CO₂ is considered the main driver of the enhanced greenhouse effect, even though water vapour is a more abundant greenhouse gas.

Water vapour concentration is controlled by temperature (evaporation/condensation), not directly by human activity — it acts as a feedback, not a forcing [1]. CO₂ is directly emitted by burning fossil fuels and deforestation, so humans control its concentration [1]. Increasing CO₂ causes initial warming, which increases evaporation, adding more H₂O and amplifying the warming (positive feedback) [1]. CO₂ also persists in the atmosphere for centuries, while H₂O cycles in/out within days, so CO₂ accumulates over time [1].
Explain [2]

Venus has a runaway greenhouse effect with surface temperature ~735 K, despite being only 30% closer to the Sun. Explain how this is possible.

Venus's atmosphere is 96% CO₂ (vs 0.04% on Earth), creating an extremely strong greenhouse effect that traps virtually all outgoing IR radiation [1]. The thick atmosphere also has high albedo (~0.75) but the greenhouse effect is so powerful that it more than compensates, raising surface temperature far above what solar input alone would produce [1].

Key Takeaways

  • Energy balance: $P_{\text{in}} = P_{\text{out}}$ for stable temperature. If $P_{\text{in}} > P_{\text{out}}$, Earth warms.
  • Greenhouse effect: short-λ solar passes through → surface absorbs → re-emits long-λ IR → GHGs absorb and re-emit IR back down → surface warmer.
  • GHGs: H₂O (~60%), CO₂ (~26%), CH₄ (~6%). N₂/O₂ are NOT greenhouse gases.
  • Natural GHE: essential, warms Earth by 33 K. Enhanced GHE: extra warming from human CO₂ emissions.
  • Albedo: fraction reflected. Snow ~0.85, ocean ~0.08. Earth average ~0.30.
  • Ice-albedo feedback: warming → ice melts → less reflection → more absorption → more warming (positive feedback).
  • Consequences: sea level rise, extreme weather, ocean acidification, ecosystem disruption.
  • CO₂ is the driver (forcing), H₂O is the amplifier (feedback). CO₂ persists for centuries.
