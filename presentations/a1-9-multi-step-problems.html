The Master Strategy for Multi-Step Problems

These problems have 2–4 stages. Each stage has its own SUVAT. The trick: the end of one stage is the start of the next.

1Read the ENTIRE problem first. Identify how many stages there are.
2Draw a timeline/diagram. Mark each stage, its start and end conditions.
3Label each stage's SUVAT separately. The $v$ of stage N becomes the $u$ of stage N+1.
4Solve stage by stage. Don't try to use one equation across multiple stages.
5Combine results. Total distance = sum of individual distances. Total time = sum of individual times.
The #1 error: Using a SUVAT equation across a boundary where acceleration changes. $v^2 = u^2 + 2as$ only works if $a$ is the SAME throughout. If $a$ changes, split into stages.
What connects stages: Time is cumulative. The final velocity of one stage = initial velocity of the next. Position carries forward.

Problem 1: Reaction + Braking 5/10

A car travels at 28 m/s. The driver sees an obstacle, takes 0.7 s to react, then brakes at $-6.0$ m s$^{-2}$. Find the total stopping distance.

Stage 1: Reaction (constant speed)

1$s_1 = v \times t = 28 \times 0.7 = \mathbf{19.6 \text{ m}}$

Stage 2: Braking (uniform deceleration)

u = 28 m/s, v = 0, a = -6.0 m/s²
2$v^2 = u^2 + 2as_2$: $0 = 784 + 2(-6.0)s_2 \implies s_2 = \frac{784}{12} = \mathbf{65.3 \text{ m}}$
$$s_{total} = 19.6 + 65.3 = \mathbf{84.9 \text{ m}}$$
Real-world: At 100 km/h (27.8 m/s), stopping distance is ~85 m — that's almost a full football field. This is why tailgating is dangerous.

Problem 2: Three-Stage Journey 6/10

A train accelerates from rest at 1.2 m s$^{-2}$ for 25 s, cruises at constant speed for 2.0 minutes, then decelerates at $-0.8$ m s$^{-2}$ to rest. Find total distance and total time.

Stage 1: Acceleration

1$v = u + at = 0 + 1.2(25) = 30$ m/s
2$s_1 = \frac{1}{2}at^2 = \frac{1}{2}(1.2)(625) = \mathbf{375 \text{ m}}$

Stage 2: Cruise at 30 m/s

3$s_2 = v \times t = 30 \times 120 = \mathbf{3600 \text{ m}}$

Stage 3: Deceleration

4$t_3 = \frac{v-u}{a} = \frac{0-30}{-0.8} = 37.5$ s
5$s_3 = \frac{v^2-u^2}{2a} = \frac{0-900}{-1.6} = \mathbf{562.5 \text{ m}}$
$$s_{total} = 375 + 3600 + 562.5 = \mathbf{4537.5 \text{ m}} \approx 4.5 \text{ km}$$
$$t_{total} = 25 + 120 + 37.5 = \mathbf{182.5 \text{ s}} \approx 3.04 \text{ min}$$

Problem 3: Free Fall + Horizontal 8/10

A ball rolls off a table at 3.0 m/s horizontally. The table is 1.2 m high. After hitting the ground, the ball bounces vertically upward at 4.0 m/s. Find: (a) time from table to ground (b) horizontal distance before bounce (c) maximum height of the bounce.

Stage 1: Projectile off table

a$h = \frac{1}{2}gt^2$: $1.2 = 4.9t^2 \implies t = 0.495$ s
b$x = u_x \times t = 3.0 \times 0.495 = \mathbf{1.49 \text{ m}}$

Stage 2: Vertical bounce

New problem: $u = 4.0$ m/s up, $a = -9.8$, $v = 0$ at top

c$v^2 = u^2 + 2as$: $0 = 16 - 19.6s$
$$s = \frac{16}{19.6} = \mathbf{0.816 \text{ m}}$$

Bounce reaches 0.82 m — less than original 1.2 m (energy lost)

Key skill: Recognise when one stage ends and another begins. Here, the bounce is a completely new problem — reset your SUVAT.

Problem 4: One Object Catching Another 8/10

Car A is at rest. Car B passes A at 20 m/s (constant speed). At that instant, A starts accelerating at 3.0 m s$^{-2}$. Find: (a) when A catches B (b) how far from the start (c) A's speed at that moment.

Setup: Both start at $x = 0$ when $t = 0$

Car A: $x_A = \frac{1}{2}(3.0)t^2 = 1.5t^2$  |  Car B: $x_B = 20t$

(a) When A catches B: $x_A = x_B$

1$1.5t^2 = 20t \implies 1.5t^2 - 20t = 0 \implies t(1.5t - 20) = 0$
2$t = 0$ (start) or $t = \frac{20}{1.5} = \mathbf{13.3 \text{ s}}$

(b) Distance:

3$x = 20 \times 13.3 = \mathbf{267 \text{ m}}$

(c) A's speed:

4$v_A = u + at = 0 + 3.0(13.3) = \mathbf{40 \text{ m/s}}$

A is now going TWICE as fast as B (40 vs 20). A doesn't just catch up — it overtakes.

Notice: At the catch-up point, A's speed (40) is exactly double B's (20). This always happens when one starts from rest with constant acceleration — at the meeting point, the accelerating object's speed is double the constant-speed object.

Problem 5: v-t Graph Analysis 8/10

The v-t graph shows a car's journey. Calculate: (a) acceleration in each phase (b) total displacement (c) total distance (d) average speed.
(a) Accelerations
A: $(20-0)/4 = +5$ m/s$^2$ | B: $0$ | C: $(-10-20)/4 = -7.5$ m/s$^2$ | D: $0$
(b) Total displacement (net area)
A: $\frac{1}{2}(4)(20) = 40$ | B: $4 \times 20 = 80$ | C: $\frac{1}{2}(4)(20+(-10)) = \frac{1}{2}(4)(10) = 20$... actually: trap area = $\frac{1}{2}(20+(-10))(4) = 20$, but split: above axis $\frac{1}{2}(2.67)(20) = 26.7$, below $\frac{1}{2}(1.33)(10) = -6.7$ | D: $4 \times (-10) = -40$
Total: 40+80+20+(-40) = +100 m
(c) Total distance (sum of |areas|)
A: 40 | B: 80 | C above: 26.7, C below: 6.7 | D: 40
Total: 40+80+26.7+6.7+40 = 193.4 m
(d) Average speed
$v_{avg} = \frac{\text{total distance}}{\text{total time}} = \frac{193.4}{16} = \mathbf{12.1 \text{ m/s}}$

IB Question Set — Full Paper 2 9/10

Context: A rocket launches vertically from rest with acceleration 15 m s$^{-2}$ for 10 s. The engine then cuts off and the rocket continues upward under gravity alone. Take $g = 9.8$ m s$^{-2}$. Ignore air resistance.
Calculate [2]

(a) Calculate the velocity of the rocket when the engine cuts off.

$v = u + at = 0 + 15(10) = \mathbf{150 \text{ m s}^{-1}}$ [2]
Calculate [2]

(b) Calculate the height at which the engine cuts off.

$s = \frac{1}{2}at^2 = \frac{1}{2}(15)(100) = \mathbf{750 \text{ m}}$ [2]
Determine [3]

(c) Determine the maximum height reached by the rocket above the ground.

Stage 2: $u = 150$, $v = 0$, $a = -9.8$
$v^2 = u^2 + 2as$: $0 = 22500 - 19.6s_2 \implies s_2 = 1148$ m
Total: $750 + 1148 = \mathbf{1898 \text{ m}}$ [3]
Calculate [2]

(d) Calculate the total time from launch to reaching maximum height.

Time in stage 2: $v = u + at$: $0 = 150 - 9.8t \implies t_2 = 15.3$ s
Total: $10 + 15.3 = \mathbf{25.3 \text{ s}}$ [2]
Determine [3]

(e) Determine the speed with which the rocket hits the ground.

Falls from 1898 m, starting from rest: $v^2 = 2(9.8)(1898) = 37200 \implies v = \mathbf{192.9 \text{ m s}^{-1}}$ [3]
(Faster than launch speed because it falls from higher than the engine-off height)

IB Question Set 2 — Two Cars 10/10

Context: Car A starts from rest and accelerates at 2.5 m s$^{-2}$ for 12 s, then maintains constant speed. Car B, 200 m ahead, travels at a constant 15 m s$^{-1}$ in the same direction. Determine whether Car A catches Car B, and if so, when and where.
Calculate [2]

(a) Find Car A's speed after the acceleration phase.

$v_A = 0 + 2.5(12) = \mathbf{30 \text{ m s}^{-1}}$ [2]
Calculate [2]

(b) Find the distance Car A covers during the acceleration phase.

$s_A = \frac{1}{2}(2.5)(144) = \mathbf{180 \text{ m}}$ [2]
Determine [3]

(c) Find the position of Car B after 12 s. What is the gap between A and B at $t = 12$ s?

Car B: $x_B = 200 + 15(12) = 380$ m from A's start
Car A: $x_A = 180$ m
Gap = $380 - 180 = \mathbf{200 \text{ m}}$ (same gap — A hasn't gained anything yet!) [3]
Determine [4]

(d) After $t = 12$ s, A cruises at 30 m/s and B at 15 m/s. Does A catch B? If so, find the time and position from A's starting point.

After $t = 12$: gap = 200 m, closing speed = $30 - 15 = 15$ m/s
Time to close: $t' = 200/15 = 13.3$ s after $t = 12$
Total time: $12 + 13.3 = \mathbf{25.3 \text{ s}}$
Position: $x_A = 180 + 30(13.3) = 180 + 400 = \mathbf{580 \text{ m}}$ from start [4]
Explain [2]

(e) Explain why the gap didn't change during the acceleration phase despite A accelerating.

A's average speed during acceleration was $\frac{0+30}{2} = 15$ m/s [1], which equals B's constant speed. So both covered the same distance in those 12 s, keeping the gap at 200 m [1]. A only starts closing the gap once its speed exceeds 15 m/s.

Common Errors Checklist

Before submitting any multi-step answer, check these:

  • Did you split the problem into stages? Each stage needs its own SUVAT.
  • Is $v$ of stage 1 used as $u$ of stage 2? The boundary condition must transfer.
  • Did you use total time or individual time? Be clear about which.
  • Sign convention consistent? If up = +, then $g = -9.8$ everywhere. Don't switch mid-problem.
  • Total distance vs displacement? Distance = sum of |stages|. Displacement = net sum (with signs).
  • Units consistent? Don't mix km/h and m/s in the same SUVAT equation.
  • Does your answer make physical sense? Speed can't be negative. Time can't be negative. Height can't exceed what the initial conditions allow.
IB marking: Error carried forward (ECF) means if your Stage 1 answer is wrong but you use it correctly in Stage 2, you still get Stage 2 marks. Show ALL working — you can't get ECF marks without it.
