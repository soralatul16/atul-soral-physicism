Task 1: Knowing & Understanding (Criterion A)
1. Read the command term
Critical

The command term tells you exactly how much to write.

Command termWhat it meansHow much
State / IdentifyGive the answer, no explanation1 sentence
DescribeSay what happens, step by step2-3 sentences
ExplainSay what happens AND why3-4 sentences with "because"
CalculateShow formula, substitution, answer3 lines minimum
AnalyseBreak it down, find patterns4-5 sentences with data
EvaluateWeigh up both sides, then judgeA paragraph with conclusion
2. The 3-line calculation rule
Easy marks

Every calculation must have 3 lines. Each line earns a mark independently.

  • Line 1: Write the formula — F = ma
  • Line 2: Substitute — F = 5 × 3
  • Line 3: Answer with units & sig figs — F = 15 N

DO: Round to 2+ sig figs. Include units. Show working even if you can do it mentally.

DON'T: Write just the answer. Skip units. Use calculator notation (1.5E4).

3. Multi-step & ECF

Wrong first step? You still earn marks on the next step if your method is correct. This is ECF (Error Carried Forward). But only if you show working.

  • Step 1: P = W/t = 500/8 = 62.5 W ← even if wrong...
  • Step 2: I = P/V = 62.5/230 = 0.27 A ← method mark earned
4. Judgment questions
High marks

DO: Reference specific data. "The value of 4.5 suggests..." State if the claim is valid with evidence.

DON'T: Give a vague opinion. Say "I agree" without reasoning. Ignore the data.

Task 2: Investigation Skills (Criteria B + C)
5. Research question
1 mark

Format: "What is the relationship between [IV] and [DV]?" or "How does [IV] affect [DV]?"

DON'T: "How does changing things affect the experiment?"

6. Hypothesis
2-4 marks

Three parts, three marks:

  • Mark 1: "If [IV] increases, then [DV] will increase/decrease"
  • Mark 2: "because [scientific reason]"
  • Mark 3: Reasoning is correct and specific (name the law or mechanism)

DON'T: Write without "because." Hypothesis doesn't need to be correct — it needs scientific reasoning.

7. Variables
3 marks
  • IV: the one you change — name it specifically
  • DV: the one you measure — name it specifically
  • CV: at least two specific things you keep the same

DON'T: Say "keep everything else the same." Don't list calculated values as DV.

8. Method design
14-16 marks

Worth more than any other single question. Examiner checks 6 things:

CheckWhat to writeCommon mistake
VariablesIV, DV, justify one CVNot justifying why CV matters
HypothesisIf-then + scienceNo "because"
EquipmentTools for IV AND DVForgetting one
MethodStep-by-step, followable"Do experiment, record results"
Data5 values, 3 repeats, averageOnly 3 values, no repeats
SafetySpecific hazard + precaution"Be careful" without a hazard

Golden rule: Could a stranger follow your method exactly? If not, add detail.

9. Tables & Graphs
C marks

Tables: Units in header only ("Mass / kg"). Data in order. Consistent decimals.

Graphs: Label axes with units. Even scale. Line of best fit (not dot-to-dot).

10. Interpreting results
  • "As [IV] increases, [DV] increases/decreases."
  • "The relationship is proportional / inversely proportional / non-linear."
  • "This is because [scientific reasoning]."
11. Evaluate hypothesis
3 marks
  • Mark 1: Quote specific data points
  • Mark 2: State whether data supports hypothesis with reasoning
  • Mark 3: Conclusion (only awarded if 1 and 2 earned)
12. Errors & improvements

Error: Name a specific one + its effect. Not "there were errors."

Improvement: Specific suggestion + why it helps. Not "do more repeats."

Task 3: Applying Science (Criterion D)

14. Maximum D marks
Game changer

Per theme row: Statement 1 + support. Statement 2 + support. = 4 marks.

Conclusion: Don't say "I think it is good" (1 mark). Say "Overall, while [benefits], the [limitations] suggest [judgment]. I believe [opinion] because [reference to arguments above]" (2 marks).

Universal Rules
16. Silent mark-killers
Memorize these
  • Missing units. Every number needs a unit. N, m, s, kg, J, W, Pa, V, A, Ω, Hz.
  • Wrong sig figs. Round to 2+ sig figs. Never 16.6666666.
  • Contradictions. Two conflicting statements = 0. Cross out mistakes.
  • Vague language. "It increases" — what increases? Name the quantity.
  • Blank responses. Formula alone = 1 mark. Blank = 0.
17. Time split
  • Task 1 (25 marks): ~30 min
  • Task 2 (50 marks): ~60 min — most time here
  • Task 3 (25 marks): ~25 min
  • Review: ~5 min — units, sig figs, blanks
18. Formula sheet

You get it on screen. Don't memorize formulas. Practice: rearranging (V=IR → I=V/R), choosing the right one, converting units before substituting.

Before You Submit
  • Did I answer what the command term asked? (State ≠ Explain)
  • Calculations show formula → substitution → answer?
  • Every number has a unit?
  • Rounded to 2+ sig figs?
  • "Evaluate" / "Discuss": gave BOTH sides?
  • Method: 5 values, 3 repeats, averaging?
  • Conclusion references earlier arguments?
  • No blank response boxes anywhere?
