StateDefine efficiency.[1]Show
Efficiency is the ratio of useful energy (or power) output to total energy (or power) input: η = useful output / total input × 100%.
CalculateAn electric motor draws 500 W and lifts a 10 kg mass at 2.0 m/s. Find the efficiency.[3]Show
Useful power = Fv = mgv = 10 × 9.81 × 2.0 = 196 W
η = 196/500 × 100% = 39.2%
The remaining 304 W is dissipated as heat and sound.
ExplainA heat pump has COP = 3.0. Explain how this is consistent with conservation of energy.[3]Show
COP 3.0 means 3 kW heat delivered per 1 kW electrical input. Not a violation — the pump draws 2 kW from the environment. Total output (3 kW) = total input (1 kW electrical + 2 kW environmental). Energy is transferred, not created.
CalculateA coal station: boiler 88%, turbine 41%, generator 95%, transformer 99%. Input = 1000 MW. Find output.[4]Show
Overall η = 0.88 × 0.41 × 0.95 × 0.99 = 33.9%
Output = 1000 × 0.339 = 339 MW
Key: efficiencies multiply, not add. 661 MW lost across stages.
DiscussSolar panel 20% vs gas turbine 40%. Is gas "better"? Discuss.[4]Show
Efficiency ≠ sustainability. Gas converts more input to output, but uses finite, CO₂-producing fuel. Solar's input (sunlight) is unlimited and free. Lower conversion from an infinite source still delivers energy indefinitely. "Better" depends on criteria: thermodynamic efficiency favours gas; sustainability favours solar.
