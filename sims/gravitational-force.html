🌍 Everything attracts everything

Every object with mass pulls on every other object with mass. You, your phone, the Moon — all connected by gravity. The attraction is always there, even if it's too small to notice.

⬆️ More mass = stronger pull

Double one object's mass and the gravitational force doubles too. The force is directly proportional to each mass.

F ∝ m

📏 Distance matters — a lot

Double the distance and the force drops to one quarter. This is called the inverse-square law — distance is squared in the denominator.

F ∝ 1/r²

🔬 G is incredibly small

The gravitational constant G = 6.674 × 10⁻¹¹ is a tiny number. That's why you don't feel gravitational pull from nearby furniture — only enormous masses like Earth produce noticeable gravity.

G = 6.674 × 10⁻¹¹ N·m²/kg²
F = G × m₁ × m₂ / r²
